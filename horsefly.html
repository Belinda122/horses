Horsefly (Tabanidae)

A large, fast-flying insect known for its painful bite. Found worldwide, it thrives in warm, humid environments, especially near livestock.

Connections

The relationship between horseflies and horses is an example of parasitism, where one party (the horsefly) benefits at the expense of the other. Female horseflies use the blood for developing eggs and can cause pain, irritation, or the spread diseases with their bite. They are also a source of food for many insect-eating birds like cattle egrets and cowbirds.

Phylum: Arthropoda Class: Insecta Order: Diptera Family: Tabanidae Genus: Tabanus
