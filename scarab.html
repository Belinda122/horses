Rainbow Scarab (Phanaeus vindex)

A colourful, bulky beetle with a shiny exoskeleton. It is native to North America and commonly found near pasture animals.

Connections

The relationship between rainbow scarabs and horses is an example of mutualism, where both parties benefit from the interaction. The scarab eat and utilize horse dung for reproduction, and by doing so provide benefits to the ecosystem. By doing this, they control the fly and parasite population (such as the horsefly population), recycle the nutrients in the dung, and clean fecal matter.

Phylum: Arthropoda Class: Insecta Order: Coleoptera Family: Scarabaeidae Genus: Phanaeus
