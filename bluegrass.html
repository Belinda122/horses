Bluegrass (Poa pratensis)

Also known as Kentucky bluegrass. A species of grass that can be found in Europe, North Asia, and North America.

Connections

The relationship between bluegrass and horses is an example of mutualism, where both parties benefit from the interaction. Bluegrass is a source of food for horses, and the act of grazing disturbs the soil and stimulates new, healthy growth. Horse fecal matter also returns nutrients to the soil, aided by the activity of dung beetles like the rainbow scarab.

Phylum: Magnoliophyta Class: Liliopsida Order: Cyperales Family: Poaceae Genus: Poa
