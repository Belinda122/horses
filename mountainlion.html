Mountain Lion (Puma concolor)

A large feline with a short beige coat, also commonly referred to as cougars, panthers, or pumas. As an adaptable species, mountain lions can be found all over the Americas in diverse environments. They are solitary animals, and are known to be territorial.

Connections

The relationship between mountain lions and horses is an example of predation, where one party benefits while the other dies. While mountain lions primarily hunt deer, they have been also been known to prey on horses and other hooved mammals.

Phylum: Chordata Class: Mammalia Order: Carnivora Family: Felidae Genus: Puma
